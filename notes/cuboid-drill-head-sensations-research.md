# Cuboid Drill — Head Sensations: Deep Research Report

Context: during 3D mental cuboid drills (gold orb jumping through 3×3×3 cells, 7-coord sequences run forward and backward), two sensations show up consistently — a "band" across the forehead and pressure/movement at the back of the head. This report explains what's happening, what contemplative traditions report, documented benefits, and how to deepen the practice.

---

## 1. Broad neuroscience of sustained vivid imagery

Visual mental imagery recruits a fronto-parieto-occipital network that overlaps substantially with perception (Pearson, *Nat Rev Neurosci* 2019, 20:624–634; Dijkstra, Bosch & van Gerven, *Trends Cogn Sci* 2019). Top-down signals from frontal and parietal cortex re-activate retinotopically organized early visual areas: small images activate posterior V1, large images more anterior V1, demonstrating depictive (not merely propositional) representation (Kosslyn, Thompson & Alpert, *Nature* 1995, 378:496–498; Kosslyn et al., *Nat Rev Neurosci* 2001).

When you mentally jump an orb across cells, the **frontal eye fields** (BA8) fire as during real saccades, but V1 response is markedly reduced versus actual eye movement (Bodis-Wollner et al., *Neurology* 1997, 49:416–420) — so the FEF "command" runs without full visual confirmation, a mismatch that recruits sustained attentional drive.

Vestibular nuclei and parieto-insular vestibular cortex are co-engaged whenever self-referenced spatial transformations are imagined; galvanic vestibular stimulation measurably alters imagined egocentric rotation but not object rotation (Lopez & Blanke, *Brain Res Rev* 2011; Falconer & Mast, *Exp Brain Res* 2012).

Sustained internally-directed cognition shifts toward the **default mode network**, especially precuneus and medial prefrontal nodes (Buckner, Andrews-Hanna & Schacter, *Ann NY Acad Sci* 2008).

At the muscular/scalp level, sustained mental effort produces a measurable EMG gradient in **frontalis and corrugator supercilii** — amplitude rises monotonically over a 20-min concentration task even in healthy controls (Waterink & van Boxtel, *Biol Psychol* 1994; Schoenen et al., *Cephalalgia* 1991). This is almost certainly the source of the "band across the brow."

## 2. Why the back of the head feels like movement

Three converging contributors:

**(a) Occipital activation.** Imagery of motion through 3D space activates posterior parietal cortex and extrastriate/occipital regions; long-term focused-attention meditators show *occipital gamma* increases (30–70 Hz) that scale with practice (Cahn, Delorme & Polich, *Cogn Process* 2010, 11:39–56) — sustained occipital drive is felt as posterior pressure.

**(b) Galea aponeurotica linkage.** The galea mechanically links *frontalis* and *occipitalis*; elevated frontalis tone is transmitted across the scalp to occipitalis, which co-contracts (Hassan & Yousaf, *J Headache Pain* 2018).

**(c) Suboccipital proprioception misread as motion.** Sustained convergence and inward gaze recruit *suboccipital* muscles, which are 36× denser in muscle spindles than gluteus maximus (Kulkarni et al., *J Anat Soc India* 2001); their proprioceptive output projects to vestibular nuclei and is interpreted as head/self motion — explaining felt "movement" without actual movement (Treleaven, *Man Ther* 2008). Imagined locomotion alone activates vestibular cortex and produces vection-like sensations (Jahn et al., *NeuroImage* 2004).

## 3. Contemplative traditions (practitioner reports + techniques)

- **Yogic** — *Trataka* and *antaranga trataka* (internal gazing) at the eyebrow center (ajna). The *Hatha Yoga Pradipika* (II.31–32) prescribes unwavering gaze "until tears flow," then internalizing the afterimage. Practitioners report a brow "band" and posterior pressure (Saraswati, *Asana Pranayama Mudra Bandha*).
- **Tibetan Vajrayana** — *Kyerim* (generation-stage deity yoga) builds elaborate 3D internal forms; Kozhevnikov et al. (*Psychol Sci* 2009, 20:645–653) found Buddhist deity practitioners showed measurable enhancement in mental rotation and visual working memory immediately post-practice. *Phowa* (Six Yogas of Naropa) explicitly visualizes a luminous bindu (drop) traveling up the central channel through crown — practitioners commonly report crown/posterior-cranial pressure (Mullin, *Six Yogas of Naropa* 1996).
- **Sufi** — *muraqaba* on the *khafi* latifa, traditionally located at the brow (Hujwiri, *Kashf al-Mahjub*; Shah, *The Sufis*).
- **Christian hesychast** — Philokalia instruction (Gregory of Sinai; Symeon the New Theologian): bow head, eyes toward the heart, breath descends with the Jesus Prayer; the *attention* is the work (Palmer, Sherrard & Ware, *The Philokalia*, vol. 4).
- **Daoist** — *huiguang* "turning the light around" in *The Secret of the Golden Flower* (Cleary trans. 1991): gaze lowered, attention reversed inward to the *yintang* point between the brows.

The cross-tradition convergence on the brow point and on inward/reverse gaze is striking; the mechanistic story (FEF + corrugator + suboccipital + occipital co-activation) plausibly explains why practitioners across cultures land on the same somatic landmarks.

## 4. Documented benefits of sustained imagery practice

- **Motor skill / sport** — PETTLEP imagery (Holmes & Collins, *J Appl Sport Psychol* 2001, 13:60–83); Smith et al. (2007/2008) showed ~22–29% putting improvement in golfers, 36% in gymnasts vs. controls.
- **Surgery** — Mental practice improves operative technical scores; Cocks et al. (*Am J Surg* 2014/2015) systematic review of 9 RCTs (n=474) found benefit in 5 of 9 trials; Arora et al. (*Ann Surg* 2011) RCT showed mental rehearsal raised OSATS scores significantly vs. textbook study.
- **Pain** — Graded Motor Imagery (left/right judgment → imagined movement → mirror) in CRPS reduces pain ~50% vs. usual care (Moseley, *Pain* 2004, 108:192–198; Bowering et al., *J Pain* 2013 meta-analysis).
- **Working memory / spatial cognition** — Mental rotation training produces durable behavioral and EEG gains at 90 days (Adams et al., *npj Sci Learn* 2025).
- **Structural plasticity** — Long-term focused-attention meditators show increased cortical thickness in right anterior insula and BA9/10 prefrontal regions (Lazar et al., *NeuroReport* 2005, 16:1893–1897); attenuates age-related thinning.

## 5. How to deepen the practice (8 evidence-grounded techniques)

1. **Fix gaze position before closing eyes** — slight downward, eyes converged inward toward the bridge of the nose. Stabilizes FEF / extraocular tone, reduces wandering microsaccades (Kosslyn 2001; *Secret of the Golden Flower*).
2. **Couple to slow nasal breath** (~5–6 bpm). Coherent breathing increases parasympathetic tone and reduces frontalis EMG creep (Brown & Gerbarg, *J Altern Complement Med* 2005).
3. **Multimodal layering** — feel the orb's weight, hear a tone at each landing. Activates more network nodes and improves recall (Holmes & Collins PETTLEP; Decety & Jeannerod 1995).
4. **Alternate eyes-open / eyes-closed cycles** (60s open on a fixation point, 60s closed reconstructing the cube). Exploits the perception/imagery shared code (Dijkstra et al. 2019) and prevents extraocular fatigue.
5. **Dose** — PETTLEP and surgical-imagery literature converge on **3–5 sessions/week of 10–20 min** producing measurable gains within 4–6 weeks (Smith et al. 2007; Arora et al. 2011). Many short reps beat one long rep.
6. **Periodic full release** — every 4–5 minutes drop the image, soften brow and jaw, blink several times. Counters the documented EMG gradient (Waterink & van Boxtel 1994).
7. **Reverse traversal as forced retrieval** — your backward-7-coord run is exactly the spaced retrieval / interleaving structure shown to consolidate spatial memory (Karpicke & Roediger, *Science* 2008).
8. **Log vividness 1–10 per session** — Pearson's group shows imagery vividness is reliably trainable when it's metacognitively monitored; binocular-rivalry paradigms confirm objective vividness gains (Pearson 2019).

---

## Bottom line

The brow "band" and posterior "movement" are predictable, mechanistically explainable byproducts of a working practice — not warning signs, but signals that frontalis/occipitalis tone and FEF/vestibular co-activation are running. Deepening means working *with* them via gaze-anchoring, breath-coupling, and dosed release rather than against them.
