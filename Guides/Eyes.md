# Eyes

> The green-eye claim. Captured in full, but read the honesty note at the bottom: this one is lore, not established fact.

Only about 2 in 100 people have green eyes, the rarest color, and most people go their whole life never locking eyes with someone who has them. The claim is that ancient civilizations independently singled them out as different: Persians, Egyptians, Sumerians, Europeans, and Africans, with no contact, different languages, and different beliefs, all landing on the same idea. The proposed mechanism is pigment: less pigment in the iris lets more light in, and more light is said to mean more sensitivity and more reception, so green-eyed people who feel everything deeply and notice what others miss might be running on built-in hardware rather than just personality.

It goes deeper into myth. The oldest written documents on earth, the Sumerian texts, are said to describe their gods with green eyes, as do Greek accounts of their oracles, the suggestion being that the first humans who ever wrote anything reached for green eyes when describing something beyond normal human range. The video also claims genetics cannot fully explain the trait: that it appeared suddenly around 10,000 years ago in one region and one population, right as the first civilizations were being built, then spread.

The final thread ties it to the third eye. Your brain has a gland at its center, the pineal gland, that contains the same light-detecting cells as your physical eyes, and produces the only psychedelic the body makes naturally (DMT). The theory offered is that whatever genetic condition keeps the physical eye open and unfiltered may also keep that internal receiver open, and that ancient peoples, without science, watched green-eyed people long enough to build temples around what they saw.

---

**Honesty note.** Unlike Nutrition and Hair, this is not a practice and most of it is not supported. What is true: green eyes are genuinely rare (roughly 2 percent). What is unverified or false: the "every ancient civilization flagged them" story, Sumerian gods being described with green eyes, the sudden 10,000-year-ago appearance, and the pineal/DMT/third-eye link are speculation, not evidence. Kept here as lore because you asked for every detail, not as fact.
