# Nutrition

> Eat foods that fill you up. Skip foods that trick you into eating more.

Every food is built from four things: **protein, fiber, fat, sugar.** Three fill you up. One empties you out. Sort everything you eat into two buckets and the whole game gets simple.

---

## The Two Categories

### 1. Fill-you-up foods (eat until satisfied)
**Protein. Fiber. Fat.** These flip your "I'm full" switch. Your body knows when to stop, so overeating is hard.

- **Protein:** meat, eggs, fish, Greek yogurt
- **Fat:** butter, olive oil, avocado, fatty cuts, cheese, nuts
- **Fiber:** leafy greens, low-sugar vegetables

Whole, single-ingredient foods. The kind that go bad if you leave them out.

### 2. Empty-you-out foods (avoid)
**Sugar, and the refined carbs that act like it.** Fiber stripped, protein stripped. They light up reward and kill satiety, so you eat and eat and never feel full.

- Anything fiber-stripped: white bread, "whole grain" rice with the fiber removed, sugary cereal
- Worst of all: **sugar + fat together** (donuts, ice cream, chips). Engineered to override your off switch.

---

## The keto note: fat is on the good list

Fat only makes you overeat when it rides shotgun with sugar. **Strip the sugar and fat becomes your ally**, not a trap. On keto, fat is your fuel, and fat-plus-protein-plus-fiber is the most satiating combination there is. The villain was never fat. It was always sugar dragging fat along with it.

---

## Memorize this

> **Protein, fiber, fat fill you up. Sugar empties you out.**

When you look at any food, ask one question: *is the sugar stripped out or packed in?* That answer tells you which bucket it's in.
